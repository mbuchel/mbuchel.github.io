WearME Project - Professor Trejos

Worked on creating a wearable brace for musculoskeletal rehabilitation, for professor Trejos. This was split up into 4 board areas, the first is motor control, second is power, third is communication, fourth is sensor input. All of these use uart for internal board to board communication, I also developed both arithmetic encoding and LMZ encoding schemes for the compression schemes of the data. I was able to hit a 2.0 compression rate which is the theoritical maximum for loseless compression.

From here I learned a habit I had was not good for development and started working on fixing this by modularizing my work and testing smaller sections instead of larger sections.

This is not open source unfortunately.
